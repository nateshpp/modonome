# ADR-010: Knowledge Packet Trust and Promotion

**Status:** Accepted
**Date:** 2026-06-24

## Context

Knowledge packets (`LEARNINGS.md` entries) are generated by the agent and staged for
owner review before promotion. Without a clear trust model, a packet with fabricated
evidence or injected instructions could propagate bad rules into the canonical prompt or
config.

## Decision

Knowledge packet trust is enforced at three points:

1. **Generation constraints.** A packet may only cite evidence that exists in the repo at
   the time of generation: gate output, reviewer comments, or CI logs. Packets that cite
   external sources, extrapolate from general knowledge, or reference events not in the
   repo's history are rejected by the checker.
2. **Validation gate.** `scripts/validate-knowledge-packet.mjs` runs on every packet
   before it is staged. It checks: schema conformance, fingerprint integrity, evidence
   field non-empty, no redacted content in body, and no banned phrases. The checker invokes
   this script as a gate; packets that fail validation are not staged.
3. **Owner promotion required.** No packet can be promoted to a canonical rule or config
   change without an explicit owner action (editing `LEARNINGS.md` to move the entry to
   the promoted section and opening a Tier 2 PR if a script or schema changes). The agent
   cannot self-promote.

The `LEARNINGS.md` cap (20 staged entries) exists to keep the review surface bounded.
When the cap is hit, the oldest unpromoted entry is archived rather than silently dropped.

## Consequences

- Prompt injection via a staged learning is structurally blocked: the agent cannot promote
  its own packets, and the owner review is a human step.
- The validation script is a Tier 2 file; any weakening of its checks requires CODEOWNERS
  approval and is therefore visible.
- Knowledge accumulated over many cycles remains auditable: the fingerprint and date on
  each entry trace it to a specific gate run.
